What the FAX is going on?

As citizens, we all have a bank account where we hold some of our money. For us to easily interact with that money, banks have created cards. By using a card, we can interact with our account to withdraw or deposit money without any struggles. As an Asset Manager, BlackRock is responsible for managing money on behalf of its clients. Our clients create Investment Accounts with BlackRock where they deposit the initial sum of money they want us to invest.
As an account owner, clients are entitled to deposit money into their accounts, called subscription, or withdraw money from their accounts, called redemption. One of the ways in which clients can subscribe or redeem is by sending BlackRock a fax message with their investment instructions.

Why would clients use fax messages?

The reason they choose to send faxes is because faxes with two signatures are legally binding and provide authorisation for this type of transaction.

Ok, fine. What is on a fax?

Faxes are different from client to client. There is not a standard layout we receive in. However, there are various amounts of information included that are common across faxes.
These are:
-The client's name-
-Two different signatures from the client-
-The investment instruction: deposit money (subscription) or withdraw money (redemption)-
-The amount of money-
-The name and code of the fund - this says the type of investment they are making -
-What region, what industry etc.-
-Bank details of where the money is coming from/to-

So what happens when BlackRock receives a fax message?

When these faxes are received in BlackRock, somebody within the 'Client Order Management' team will get this fax in their email as an image and analyse it. They will check if the signatures are different and enter all the information (listed above) into the investment system to successfully complete the transaction.
